This Acceptable Use Policy ("Policy") applies to use of Blueprint's website, hosted application, APIs, generated outputs, documentation, and related services (the "Service"). This Policy is part of the [Terms of Service](terms-of-service.md).

## 1. Safety-First Scope

Blueprint is intended for low-voltage maker electronics, educational prototypes, and early design exploration. You may not use the Service to create or facilitate unsafe, illegal, abusive, or rights-violating activity.

## 2. Prohibited Hardware and Physical-World Uses

You may not use the Service to design, optimize, instruct, source, or deploy:

- weapons, explosives, traps, harmful devices, or systems intended to injure people, animals, or property;
- mains AC, high-voltage, high-current, unsafe battery, or high-power energy systems without qualified professional oversight;
- medical, diagnostic, therapeutic, life-support, or health-critical systems;
- automotive, aviation, railway, maritime, industrial control, public infrastructure, emergency response, or other safety-critical systems;
- systems for unlawful surveillance, stalking, credential theft, evasion, sabotage, or physical intrusion;
- devices intended to bypass safety interlocks, emissions controls, access controls, locks, alarms, or security systems;
- products for sale or deployment without required engineering review, testing, certifications, labeling, and regulatory compliance.

## 3. Prohibited Content and Conduct

You may not:

- violate laws, regulations, sanctions, export controls, court orders, or third-party rights;
- submit content that infringes intellectual property, privacy, publicity, confidentiality, or contract rights;
- upload malware, exploit code, harmful files, or content intended to compromise systems;
- attempt to probe, scan, or test the Service except as allowed by the [Security and Vulnerability Disclosure Policy](security-policy.md);
- bypass rate limits, access controls, safety controls, payment controls, model restrictions, or usage limits;
- scrape, harvest, or collect data except as permitted by documented APIs;
- impersonate others, misrepresent affiliation, or engage in phishing, spam, fraud, or deceptive practices;
- use the Service to generate hateful, harassing, exploitative, sexualized minor, or violent extremist content;
- submit personal data about others without a lawful basis and required consent;
- use outputs as final professional engineering advice or safety certification.

## 4. AI and Provider Abuse

You may not use the Service to abuse model providers, image providers, cloud providers, marketplaces, or integrations, including by:

- sending prompts designed to extract secrets, credentials, system prompts, private data, or provider internals;
- using stolen, leaked, or unauthorized API keys;
- routing data to third-party providers in violation of their terms;
- evading model safety restrictions or usage policies;
- creating excessive automated traffic that degrades the Service.

## 5. Responsible Use of Outputs

You are responsible for reviewing outputs before use. At minimum, independently verify:

- component ratings, datasheets, pinouts, current draw, voltage levels, and thermal limits;
- wiring, polarity, grounding, fusing, battery protection, and enclosure safety;
- supplier reliability, substitutions, lead times, authenticity, and compliance;
- legal, export, environmental, accessibility, and product safety requirements.

## 6. Enforcement

We may investigate suspected violations and may remove content, restrict features, throttle usage, suspend accounts, terminate access, preserve records, or report activity to authorities where appropriate.

## 7. Reporting

Report abuse or safety concerns to team@caid-technologies.com. Report security vulnerabilities to team@caid-technologies.com.
