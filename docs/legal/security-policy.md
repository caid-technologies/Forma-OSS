Blueprint welcomes good-faith security research and vulnerability reports. This policy explains how to report issues safely.

## 1. Reporting

Send vulnerability reports to team@caid-technologies.com.

Include, when possible:

- affected URL, API route, package, or component;
- clear reproduction steps;
- impact and severity estimate;
- screenshots, logs, or proof-of-concept details;
- whether you accessed any data that was not yours.

Do not include secrets, personal data, or exploit code beyond what is needed to understand the issue.

## 2. In Scope

Unless we publish a more specific scope, reports may cover:

- the public Blueprint web application;
- documented Blueprint APIs;
- authentication, authorization, project access, and integration handling;
- public repository code maintained by Blueprint.

## 3. Out of Scope

The following are out of scope unless we give written permission:

- denial-of-service or load testing;
- social engineering, phishing, or physical attacks;
- attacks against third-party providers or suppliers;
- accessing, modifying, deleting, or exfiltrating data that is not yours;
- automated scanning that degrades the Service;
- reports based only on missing headers or low-impact configuration findings without exploitability.

## 4. Safe Harbor

If you act in good faith, follow this policy, avoid privacy violations and disruption, and report issues promptly, Blueprint will not pursue legal action against you for the research itself. This safe harbor does not apply to unlawful conduct, extortion, data theft, public disclosure before resolution, or activity outside this policy.

## 5. Coordinated Disclosure

Give us a reasonable opportunity to investigate and fix issues before public disclosure. We will try to acknowledge reports within [ACKNOWLEDGMENT WINDOW, e.g. 3 business days] and provide updates as appropriate.

## 6. Bounty

Unless a separate bounty program is announced, Blueprint does not offer payment, rewards, swag, or compensation for reports.
